Self-pickup skips most of the delivery form. There's no venue address, no event dates, no execution contact — just who's collecting, when, and when you expect to return the items. Three steps, usually under a minute.

<FeatureFlagNote flag="enable_self_pickup">
    This article applies when your company has self-pickup enabled and you picked **Self-Pickup** on
    the mode picker. See [Choosing delivery or self-pickup](../ordering/choosing-delivery-or-pickup)
    for how to get here.
</FeatureFlagNote>

The three steps, in order:

1. **Review Items** — confirm the cart.
2. **Collection Details** — collector info + pickup window + optional expected return.
3. **Review & Submit** — scrollable summary with the Submit button.

### Collector

The collector is the person who physically walks into the warehouse. Three fields:

- **Collector Name** _(required)_ — full name, auto-filled from your profile.
- **Collector Phone** _(required)_ — auto-filled from your profile. The warehouse calls this number if they can't locate the collector on pickup day.
- **Collector Email** _(optional)_ — auto-filled from your profile. Used for pickup confirmations if provided.

Edit any of these if someone else is doing the collection on your behalf — a driver, a colleague, a courier.

### Pickup window

The time range during which you'll arrive at the warehouse. Three inputs on one row:

- **Date** — when you're collecting.
- **From** — earliest arrival time.
- **To** — latest arrival time (same day).

Treat this as a range, not an exact time. The warehouse team plans around the window and has your items staged by the **From** time.

### Expected return (optional)

When you'll bring the items back. If you leave this blank, the system applies a default return window based on your tenant's configuration. If you know your specific return date, fill it in — it gives logistics better visibility for scheduling incoming stock.

### Notes (optional)

A free-text field at the bottom of the form for anything the warehouse should know: vehicle type you'll arrive in, special access instructions, two collectors, etc.

Click **Continue** once the required fields are filled.

Two summary cards on one page:

- **Collector** — name, phone, collection date + time range, expected return (if set), notes (if set).
- **Items** — every cart line recapped with quantity.

If you spot something wrong, use **Back** to step backward and fix it. Your inputs are preserved on every step.

Click **Submit Pickup Request**. The portal fires a toast on success and takes you to the new self-pickup's detail page under <PortalLink to="/self-pickups" label="/self-pickups" />. Your cart is cleared automatically on submit.

<Callout kind="heads-up" title="Pricing and availability are confirmed later">
    Submitting doesn't reserve the items. The logistics team reviews the request, confirms
    availability against your pickup window, and generates a quote. You'll review and approve that
    quote before the pickup is locked in — same pattern as a delivery order.
</Callout>

## Switching to delivery mid-flow

On Step 1 (Review Items), the **Back** button reads **Switch to Delivery** instead of Back. Clicking it returns you to the [mode picker](../ordering/choosing-delivery-or-pickup) with your cart intact. Use this if you realise you do need delivery after all.
