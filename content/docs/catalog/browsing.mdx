The catalog is where you pick what you want on your order. It groups inventory at three layers — individual **units**, reusable **assets** (also called families internally), and pre-bundled **collections** — so you can start from whichever level matches how you think.

## The three views

The tab strip at the top of the catalog switches between three views of the same
underlying inventory.

- **All** — every asset and every collection mixed into one grid. Best when
  you're exploring and don't know exactly what you need yet.
- **Assets** — asset-level cards only. Each card represents a whole family of
  interchangeable units. Best when you're looking for something specific like
  a chair type or a backdrop.
- **Collections** — only bundled collections. Best when you want a pre-curated
  package and don't want to piece it together yourself.

<Callout kind="tip" title="Assets or collections?">
    Every **collection** is made up of assets and their underlying units. You can order the whole
    collection with one click, or dip into a specific asset on its own — same inventory, two ways
    in.
</Callout>

## Reading a catalog card

Each card shows the essentials without you having to click in:

- **Name + image.**
- **Brand + category** pills so you know what flavour it is.
- For an asset: **&lt;available&gt; available / &lt;total&gt;** so you know what's
  actually in the warehouse right now.
- For a collection: **&lt;N&gt; items** with a hint of which assets are inside.
- A **condition flag** if any items in the group are flagged ORANGE or RED —
  covered in the [Conditions article](../catalog/conditions).

Clicking anywhere on a card takes you into the detail page for that item.

## Pagination

The catalog shows 24 results per page. If you have more, use the **Previous** /
**Next** buttons at the bottom — the page counter sits between them.

<Callout kind="heads-up" title="Filters survive pagination">
    Moving through pages keeps your filters active. If you want to reset, hit **Clear filters** near
    the search bar.
</Callout>
