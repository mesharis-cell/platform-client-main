Every individual unit in the catalog has a **condition**, which tells you how ready-to-ship that unit is. The catalog surfaces condition at every level — on cards, on family detail pages, and on asset detail pages — because it changes what happens when you add the unit to your order.

## The three states

- **GREEN** — ready to use as-is. No notes, no extra steps. The default.
- **ORANGE** — minor issues that are acceptable if you're fine with them. The
  portal will ask you at checkout whether you want the item **fixed before
  the event** or **used as-is**.
- **RED** — needs repair before it can ship. You can still add RED units to
  your order, but the portal runs a **feasibility check** to make sure your
  team can refurbish them before your event start date.

## Where you see condition

### On a catalog card

Cards show a small condition badge when any unit in the family is flagged
ORANGE or RED — for example, _"2 need repair"_ for two RED units, or
_"1 minor issue"_ for one ORANGE. GREEN-only families don't show a badge.

### On an asset detail page

The asset detail page has a dedicated **Condition & Maintenance** tab that
shows:

- The current condition (GREEN / ORANGE / RED)
- **Condition notes** — a free-text description from the team that inspected
  the unit
- **Refurb days estimate** — for ORANGE/RED units, how many days of work are
  needed before it's shippable
- The latest condition photos captured during scanning
- A full **Condition History** timeline if the unit has been re-assessed

## What happens when you order a non-GREEN item

- **ORANGE.** The item is added to your cart with condition info preserved.
  At checkout, the **Maintenance Decision Center** asks you for a choice per
  ORANGE item:
    - **Fix before event** — the platform team refurbishes the unit.
    - **Use as-is** — the unit ships flagged, you accept it as it is.
- **RED.** The item is added but checkout runs a **maintenance feasibility
  check** using the refurb-days estimate and your event start date. If the
  timeline doesn't work, checkout tells you the earliest feasible event date.

<Callout kind="heads-up" title="You can't skip a maintenance decision">
    If your cart has any ORANGE items when you open checkout, you'll be blocked from submitting
    until every ORANGE item has a decision. The Review step lists them so nothing slips through.
</Callout>
