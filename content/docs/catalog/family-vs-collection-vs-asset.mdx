The catalog groups inventory into three concepts: **assets**, **families**, and **collections**. They all represent items you can put on an order, but they live at different levels of aggregation. Understanding the difference makes filtering and ordering a lot faster.

## Asset — a single item

An **asset** is the smallest unit — one specific thing in the warehouse. One
chair. One LED screen. One backdrop panel. An asset has a unique serial, a
physical location, and a current condition.

You rarely need to think at the asset level unless the specific identity
matters (for example, you know the event venue has a restriction that rules
out certain units).

## Family — a group of similar assets

A **family** is a group of interchangeable assets. They look identical, they're
priced the same, and you usually don't care which specific unit you get — you
care about **how many**. Families have a **stock mode** that tells the catalog
how to surface availability:

- **Pooled** — the family tracks by count ("20 of 30 available"). Perfect for
  bulk items like chairs where one unit is as good as the next.
- **Serialized** — each unit in the family is tracked individually ("3 of 4
  available", with a specific unit shown as unavailable). Used when the
  physical identity matters — different assembly, different condition, or a
  different history.

## Asset detail

Clicking an individual unit on a serialized family takes you to its **asset
detail** page — the single-unit view. Here you see the full spec, the image
gallery, any condition notes, the refurb-days estimate if it's flagged ORANGE
or RED, and three tabs with deeper information: **Usage Report**, **Condition
History**, and **Versions**.

## Collection — a bundle

A **collection** is a curated package of items — multiple families grouped
together for a specific use case. The seeded example is _Corporate Event
Package_, which pulls chairs, backdrop panels, and LED screens into one bundle
with sensible default quantities.

Collections give you two ways to order:

- **Order the whole collection** with the default quantities.
- **Customize** the selection — toggle items off, or adjust quantities —
  before adding to your cart. Useful when you only need part of a package.

<Callout kind="tip" title="Which view should I start from?">
    If you know exactly what you need, start from the **Families** tab. If you're outfitting a
    standard event, start from **Collections** — you'll save clicks.
</Callout>
