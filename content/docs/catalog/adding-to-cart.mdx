Your cart sits above every catalog page as a floating button in the bottom-right corner. Anything you add from any detail page lands in the cart and stays there — even across browser refreshes — until you remove it or submit an order.

## Add a family or asset

From a family detail page (pooled or serialized) or an asset detail page:

<StepList>
    <Step n={1} title="Pick your quantity">
        Use the **+** and **−** buttons on the quantity stepper. The portal
        caps you at the current available quantity for that asset — you can't
        add more than the warehouse has.
    </Step>
    <Step n={2} title="Click Add to Cart">
        A toast appears confirming the add. The floating cart button in the
        bottom-right lights up with the new item count.
    </Step>
    <Step n={3} title="Keep browsing or open the cart">
        Either continue to another family to add more, or click the floating
        cart button to review what you've got.
    </Step>
</StepList>

<Callout kind="tip" title="Adding the same item twice">
If the asset is already in your cart, clicking Add to Cart with a new quantity
*merges* — your cart shows the new total, not two separate entries. The
original condition and any maintenance decisions carry forward.
</Callout>

## Add a whole collection

On a collection detail page you have two choices:

- **Customize Collection** — opens a dialog where you can toggle items off
  and tweak quantities before adding.
- Add the whole bundle at default quantities straight through.

The customize dialog is how you do things like *"the events package minus the
LED screens we already own"* without manually adding each family.

## Open the cart

The floating button in the bottom-right corner shows your current item count
on a red badge. Click it to open the cart panel.

In the panel you can:

- **Adjust the quantity** on any line with the + and − buttons.
- **Remove an item** with the trash icon.
- **Clear the whole cart** from the footer menu.
- **Proceed to Checkout** when you're ready to submit.

## What your cart remembers

The cart is saved in your browser — it survives refreshes, browser restarts,
and even syncs live between open tabs of the portal. A few points worth
knowing:

- **Per-item condition and maintenance decisions** are kept alongside the
  quantity.
- **If an item's availability drops** between when you added it and when you
  open checkout, the Review step flags the issue before you can submit.
- **The cart belongs to the browser**, not the account — if you sign in from
  a different device, you start with an empty cart.

<Callout kind="heads-up" title="One cart per browser">
Signing out doesn't clear the cart by itself, but the contents are tied to the
device and browser you were using. Switching devices means rebuilding the cart
from scratch.
</Callout>
