Once your order moves into fulfillment, logistics scans items at every handover point: outbound from the warehouse, loaded onto the truck, arrived on-site, during derig, onto the return truck, and back at the warehouse. Every scan becomes an entry you can see on the order page — with photos, condition, and notes.

## When it appears

The **Scan Activity** section shows up on the order detail page once the order is at any of these statuses:

- **CONFIRMED** — first scans start appearing as prep begins
- **IN_PREPARATION / READY_FOR_DELIVERY** — outbound scans and truck photos
- **IN_TRANSIT / DELIVERED** — on-site capture
- **IN_USE / AWAITING_RETURN** — mid-event and derig photos
- **RETURN_IN_TRANSIT / CLOSED** — return truck photos and inbound scans

Before CONFIRMED, the section isn't rendered — there's nothing to show yet.

Two parts:

- **Summary counters** (three cards) — the count of outbound scans,
  inbound scans, and captures (photo-only events like truck photos or
  derig capture).
- **Scan Timeline** — a chronological list of every scan event, each
  one a full card with type badge, asset info, timestamp, photos, and
  notes.

## Why it's useful

Scan activity gives you a complete audit trail of your order's physical journey. Common uses:

- **Confirming delivery happened** and at what exact time.
- **Inspecting derig photos** to see how items were broken down at the
  end of your event.
- **Investigating discrepancies** — "the team said one panel was
  damaged on return; let me see the photo."
- **Settling disputes** — scans are timestamped by the actual scanner
  (a human name, not the system).
