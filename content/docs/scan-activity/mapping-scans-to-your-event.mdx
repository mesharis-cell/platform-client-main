Once you've seen a few orders go through, you'll start to recognise the shape of the scan timeline. This article gives you the mental model so new orders feel familiar.

## The six beats of a typical order

Every fulfillment order hits these six beats, in order:

### 1. Pre-event: Outbound Scans (T−2 to T−1 days)

A day or two before your event start, the warehouse team starts
scanning items out. You'll see multiple **Outbound Scan** entries on
the timeline — one per asset group. Condition is GREEN; nothing
unusual.

### 2. Dispatch: Outbound Truck Photos (T−1 day / day-of)

Once the truck is loaded, the team takes photos. **Outbound Truck
Photos** entry captures them. No condition, just visual proof the
truck left packed.

### 3. On-site: On Site Capture (event day)

Arrives at venue. After install, or partway through the event, the
crew takes check-in photos. **On Site Capture** entry — usually with a
short note like "Setup complete, tested" or "Mid-event check, all
running".

### 4. Teardown: Derig Capture (event end)

As items come down, the team photographs them. **Derig Capture**
entry — often with a condition note if anything got flagged during
the event.

### 5. Return dispatch: Return Truck Photos (post-event)

Items are loaded back onto a return truck. **Return Truck Photos** —
mirror of step 2.

### 6. Back at warehouse: Inbound Scan (post-event)

Items are scanned back in. **Inbound Scan** — with condition on return.
Most orders finish here with GREEN condition and no discrepancy, and
the order flips to **CLOSED** shortly after.

## How the timeline looks visually

The Scan Timeline card on your order page lists every scan with its
type badge and timestamp. Reading top-to-bottom (most recent first),
you'll typically see:

```
Inbound Scan            — post-event
Return Truck Photos     — post-event
Derig Capture           — event end
On Site Capture         — event day
Outbound Truck Photos   — dispatch
Outbound Scan × N       — prep
```

Multiple Outbound Scans at the bottom (one per asset group), then the
rest of the beats at one each.

## Exceptions

A few situations break the typical shape:

- **Multi-day events** might have several On Site Capture entries —
  one per check-in.
- **Derig capture** can be split across multiple entries if teardown
  happens over hours.
- **Inbound with a discrepancy** — the entry will have the amber
  banner. See [Discrepancies](../scan-activity/discrepancies).
- **Partial returns** — rare, but if some items stay on-site longer,
  inbound scans are split across multiple entries at different times.
