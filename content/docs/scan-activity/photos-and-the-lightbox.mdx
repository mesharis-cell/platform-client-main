Most scan types attach photos. Truck photos attach a batch of wide shots of the loaded truck; derig capture attaches close-ups of how items looked at teardown; inbound scans attach damage photos when anything's flagged. Here's how to browse them.

At the bottom of every scan card that has photos, you'll see a small
header like **3 photo(s)** with a camera icon, followed by a 3-column
grid of thumbnails. Every thumbnail is clickable.

## The lightbox

Clicking any thumbnail opens a full-screen lightbox viewer:

- **Navigate** between photos with on-screen arrows or the keyboard
  (left/right).
- **Close** with the X button or by pressing Escape.
- **Photo description** shows beneath the image if the scanner added
  one — for example, _"Stage area before pack-down"_ or _"Dent on
  lower corner of panel"_.

## What to look for

By photo type:

- **Truck photos** — quick check that items were packed correctly.
  Not usually something you'll scrutinise unless a packing issue
  comes up.
- **On-site capture** — visual confirmation install was completed.
- **Derig capture** — before-pack-down shots of items; the condition
  note on the card pairs with what's visible in the photo.
- **Inbound with discrepancy** — the damage photo. This is the one
  to zoom in on.
