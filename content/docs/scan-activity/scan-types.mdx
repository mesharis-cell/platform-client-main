Your order generates up to six kinds of scan events over its lifetime. This article walks through each — what it captures, when it happens, and what you should look at on the entry.

## Outbound Scan

**Colour:** primary · **When:** during prep, before the truck leaves
the warehouse.

Outbound scans record individual items leaving the warehouse floor
heading for your event. Each outbound scan captures:

- **Which asset(s)** and how many (for pooled items).
- **Condition at departure** — usually GREEN.
- **Actor** — the logistics team member who did the scan.
- **Notes** — optional, typically just the team member confirming
  nothing unusual.

Expect one outbound scan per asset-group in your order, at some point
between CONFIRMED and IN_TRANSIT.

## Outbound Truck Photos

**Colour:** blue · **When:** after loading is complete, before the
truck departs.

A batch of photos of the loaded truck so you have a visual record of
how your items were packed. No asset condition is captured here — it's
a photo-only event. Useful to reference if a packing dispute comes up
later.

## On Site Capture

**Colour:** pink · **When:** during the event, typically at the end of
install and/or mid-event check-in.

Photos taken at your venue, often with a short note like _"Setup
complete, all screens tested"_. Gives you confirmation that things
arrived installed and working.

## Derig Capture

**Colour:** purple · **When:** at teardown, end of your event.

Photos captured as items are being taken down, plus a condition note
per item if anything was flagged. If something was damaged during the
event, this is where you'll see it first — usually with a photo.

## Return Truck Photos

**Colour:** orange · **When:** during the return journey's departure —
items loaded onto the return truck.

Mirror of Outbound Truck Photos, but for the return leg. Again, no
condition; just visual record of how items were packed for return.

## Inbound Scan

**Colour:** secondary · **When:** when items are back at the warehouse
and being scanned in.

The last scan in a typical lifecycle. Captures:

- **Condition on return** — the moment of truth. If something is
  damaged, this is where it gets flagged.
- **Discrepancy reason** — if the inbound scan doesn't match what
  went out (broken, lost, or other), this field explains.
- **Photos** — typically of any damage.
- **Notes** — usually the damage description.

<Callout kind="tip" title="The pair you'll pay most attention to">
    **Outbound + Inbound** are the most diagnostic pair. Compare condition and counts between them
    to see if anything changed during your event. The capture-only scans (truck photos, on-site,
    derig) are supplementary visual context.
</Callout>

## Mapped to the lifecycle

| Scan type             | Usually happens at status           |
| --------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Outbound Scan         | IN_PREPARATION → READY_FOR_DELIVERY |
| Outbound Truck Photos | READY_FOR_DELIVERY → IN_TRANSIT     |
| On Site Capture       | DELIVERED / IN_USE                  |
| Derig Capture         | IN_USE → AWAITING_RETURN            |
| Return Truck Photos   | AWAITING_RETURN → RETURN_IN_TRANSIT |
| Inbound Scan          | RETURN_IN_TRANSIT → CLOSED          |
