When an inbound scan finds something that doesn't match what went out — a broken panel, a missing chair, an item in unexpected condition — the scanner records a **discrepancy reason**. It shows on the scan card as an amber banner; it also usually triggers follow-up work on the logistics side.

The banner sits immediately below the main scan info and the notes.
Its text reads **Discrepancy: REASON** with one of three reason codes.

## The three reasons

- **BROKEN** — the item came back but is damaged or non-functional.
  Accompanied by a photo and a note describing what's wrong.
- **LOST** — the item didn't come back at all. Scanner recorded the
  gap.
- **OTHER** — anything that doesn't fit the first two. The note on
  the same card explains what happened.

Every discrepancy case also usually carries a scanner note that
spells out the context in plain English.

## What happens after

A discrepancy usually triggers one or both of these:

- **A service request** is opened against the returned asset for
  maintenance or write-off. If it's linked to your order, it shows
  on the [Linked Service Requests](../order-page/linked-service-requests)
  card.
- **A stock adjustment** is logged on the asset's inventory ledger —
  this is internal to the platform team and doesn't appear on the
  client portal.

<Callout kind="heads-up" title="Discrepancies aren't automatic charges">
    Seeing "Discrepancy: BROKEN" on your scan timeline doesn't mean you're being charged extra. Any
    follow-up billing goes through a service request quote that you approve or decline separately.
    No silent surprises on your invoice.
</Callout>
