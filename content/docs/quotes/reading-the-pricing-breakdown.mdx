The pricing breakdown is the heart of your quote. Knowing how to read it makes the approve-or-decline decision a lot faster. Everything below is shown in AED.

From top to bottom:

1. **Line items** — one row per billable service or item group. Each row
   shows a plain-English description on the left and an amount in AED
   on the right.
2. **Subtotal** — the sum of every visible line.
3. **VAT row** (only when applicable) — either a subtracted-in amount
   or a note that VAT is already rolled into line totals, shown with
   the configured rate (e.g. 5.00%).
4. **TOTAL** — the bold, primary-coloured bottom line. This is what
   you'll be invoiced.

## Common line items

The pricing engine generates lines based on what's in your order and which platform-rate-card items apply. You'll commonly see:

- **Basic Assembly** — crew time for assembly on-site.
- **Loading / Unloading** — warehouse handling.
- **Picking & Handling (X.XXX m³)** — a "base operations" line that
  scales with the total volume of your cart. Sometimes shown as a
  label-only row without a per-line amount — its value is baked into
  the subtotal.
- **Transport** — vehicle and route costs.
- **Reskin / Rebrand** — custom branding work per item.
- **Custom Line** — anything one-off the logistics team added
  manually, with an explanatory label.

<Callout kind="tip" title="Label-only lines are by design">
If you see a line that has a label ("Picking & Handling (1.020 m³)")
but no amount on the right, the amount is computed into the subtotal
even though the per-line number isn't displayed. No double-counting
is happening.
</Callout>

## What's never shown

By design the pricing breakdown **only shows client-safe pricing**. Things the portal intentionally hides:

- **Buy prices / platform cost** — what the platform pays its suppliers.
- **Margin percentage** — the markup applied to reach the sell price.
- **Internal rate-card detail** — the formula the logistics team used
  to generate the quote.

You'll never see these on any client surface — the order detail page,
the PDF cost estimate, the approved-quote snapshot. If a number makes
it to your view, it's final and invoice-ready.

## VAT

If your company has VAT configured on its account, the pricing
breakdown will include a dedicated VAT row between Subtotal and TOTAL.
The display flips between two formats:

- **VAT exclusive** — "VAT (5.00%) ... 5.32 AED" with an explicit
  amount added on top of the subtotal.
- **VAT inclusive** — "VAT included (5.00%)" with no separate amount,
  because the line totals already have VAT baked in.

Either way, the TOTAL at the bottom is the final number you pay.
