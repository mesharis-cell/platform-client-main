If the quoted pricing doesn't work — too high, wrong scope, event cancelled on your end — the portal has a formal decline path. Decline closes the order; it doesn't go back to pricing review.

## Step 1 — Click Decline Quote

From the Quote Review section on the order page, click **Decline Quote**. A confirmation dialog opens.

## Step 2 — Explain why

The **Decline reason** textarea is required, with a 10-character
minimum. Write a short note that tells logistics what happened — a few
useful templates:

- *"Price came in higher than our approved budget for this event."*
- *"Event moved to a different vendor; order no longer needed."*
- *"Venue cancelled on our side. Sorry for the churn."*
- *"Need to reduce scope — will rebuild a smaller cart and start again."*

The reason is visible to the platform's logistics and admin teams
permanently — it shows on the internal order list and on the decline
timeline row. It's not visible to other clients.

<Callout kind="heads-up" title="Declining is terminal">
Once declined, the order can't be "un-declined" from the portal — it
stops at DECLINED and doesn't re-enter pricing review. If you want to
proceed after talking with the team, build a new order.
</Callout>

## Step 3 — Click Decline Quote

The button stays disabled until you type enough characters. Click it
and the dialog submits.

## What happens after

- **Status** flips to **DECLINED**. The status hero shows "Quote
  Declined" and your reason is captured on the Quote Summary card.
- **No asset reservations** are made. Everything in the cart for that
  order is released.
- **The cost estimate PDF** stays available — you can still download it
  for your records.
