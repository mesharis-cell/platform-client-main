Once logistics has sent your quote, a **Download Cost Estimate** button appears in the right sidebar of the order page. Clicking it downloads a print-ready PDF of the same pricing you see in the portal, formatted for forwarding to finance or attaching to a PO.

The button sits at the very top of the right-hand sidebar. While the
download runs, the label changes briefly to **Downloading…** then goes
back to its idle state.

## When it's available

The button appears as soon as your order reaches **QUOTED** and stays
available through the rest of the lifecycle. Specifically it's
visible for every status from QUOTED through CLOSED, including DECLINED
— so you can always reference the original quoted pricing even on
orders you didn't end up approving.

## Filename

The downloaded file is named
`cost-estimate-{order-id}.pdf` — for example
`cost-estimate-ORD-DEMO-002.pdf`. Matches the on-portal ID so you
can find it easily in your downloads folder later.

## What the PDF contains

The PDF follows the same client-safe pricing principle as the on-portal
view. You'll see:

- **Header** — platform legal name, address, and the project reference
    - issue date.
- **Client + Installation** — your company, contact name, email, phone,
  event start/end, venue name/city/address.
- **Items table** — serial number, asset name, quantity, and notes
  (handling tags or collection name).
- **Cost breakdown** — the same line items as the portal, subtotal,
  VAT if applicable, and the final **Estimated Total**.
- **Notes** — a short disclaimer that the PDF is an estimate, not a
  final invoice.
- **Footer** — platform contact details.

<Callout kind="heads-up" title="Regenerated on every revision">
    If logistics revises the quote, a fresh PDF is produced. Downloading again always gives you the
    current quote, not a cached earlier version. Old PDFs you'd already saved stay accurate to the
    quote they captured; they just don't reflect later revisions.
</Callout>
