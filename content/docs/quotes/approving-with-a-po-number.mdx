Approving a quote does three things in one click: locks in the pricing, attaches your PO number to the order, and kicks off asset reservation and preparation. This article walks you through the dialog.

## Step 1 — Click Accept Quote

From the Quote Review section of the order page, click **Accept Quote**. A confirmation dialog opens.

The dialog restates the order total in AED so you can't approve the
wrong amount by accident.

## Step 2 — Enter your PO number

The **Purchase Order Number** field is required. Type the PO reference
your finance system uses. It gets stored against the order and:

- **Shows on the order detail page** under the order ID, from the
  moment of approval forward.
- **Appears on the final invoice** the platform issues later.
- **Is returned on every API call** about the order, for your internal
  systems.

<Callout kind="tip" title="Make sure the PO is open on your side first">
    The platform will invoice against this PO — double-check it's active in your procurement system
    and has enough budget allocated before you click submit.
</Callout>

closes, a green success toast appears — **Quote accepted! Your order is
confirmed.** — and the order page updates in place.

Three changes are visible immediately:

- **Status hero** flips from "Quote Ready" → **"Order Confirmed"**.
- **PO number** appears on the order ID card.
- **Quote Review** section collapses into a **Quote Summary** — same
  pricing, read-only, with an "Approved on DATE" timestamp.

Preparation kicks off on the logistics side — asset reservations go
active, and the order moves to IN_PREPARATION as soon as the team
picks it up.

<Callout kind="heads-up" title="PO cannot be edited from the portal">
    Once entered on approval, the PO is locked. If you need to change it, ask your platform admin —
    they can update it on the backend.
</Callout>
