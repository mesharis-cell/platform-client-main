Sometimes the first quote isn't the final one. Logistics might revise pricing because the venue needs specialised transport, a line item got missed, or a cost changed between quoting and your approval. When that happens, the order is **re-quoted** and you get a fresh decision to make.

## How you're notified

You'll get a **"Revised Quote"** email from the platform with the order ID in the subject line. The body shows:

- The **previous total** and the **new total** side-by-side.
- A short **reason** the team provided for the revision.
- A direct link back to the order page.

Revised quotes can happen before or after you approved the original
quote — if logistics changes pricing mid-preparation, you'll be asked
to re-approve.

## What changes on the order page

- The order goes back to **QUOTED** (even if it was CONFIRMED before).
- The Quote Review section re-opens with the **new pricing**.
- A subtle banner notes the quote was revised and shows the reason.
- The **Accept Quote** and **Decline Quote** buttons come back.

## What you do

Exactly the same as a first-time quote:

- Review the [pricing breakdown](../quotes/reading-the-pricing-breakdown).
- Decide: [approve with your PO](../quotes/approving-with-a-po-number)
  (re-enter the PO if asked — it may be pre-filled from the first
  approval) or [decline with a reason](../quotes/declining-a-quote).

<Callout kind="heads-up" title="Re-approving keeps your PO intact">
If you're re-approving a revised quote on an order that was already
confirmed, the PO number carries over by default. You can overwrite
it if your finance system needs a different reference for the revised
amount.
</Callout>

## When to expect a revision

Most orders are quoted once and approved without revision. Revisions
tend to happen when:

- You updated the cart or event dates after submission (the platform
  recomputes).
- A line item had to be added manually (e.g., a permit charge).
- A cost went up between the original quote and your approval.
- A reskin or rebrand request was added post-quote.

In every case, the platform team is required to include a reason so
you don't see a silent price change.
