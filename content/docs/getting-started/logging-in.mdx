Your administrator provisions every portal account. Once they've added you, you'll have an email address and password you can use to sign in — no self-registration, no invitation link.

## Before you start

You'll need:

- **The tenant URL** your admin shared with you (something like
  `https://client.yourcompany.kadence.app`).
- **The email address** registered on your account — the exact one your
  admin set up, not an alias.
- **Your password.** If you don't have one yet, or you've forgotten it,
  you can set one via <PortalLink to="/forgot-password" label="Forgot Password" />
  on the login screen.

<Callout kind="heads-up" title="Accounts are admin-provisioned">
The sign-up page exists but is disabled. If your credentials don't work
or you can't log in, ask your administrator to create an account for you
or check the role on your profile.
</Callout>

<StepList>
    <Step n={1} title="Open the tenant URL">
        Paste the portal URL your admin shared into your browser. You'll
        land on the login screen — the one titled **Access / Control**.
    </Step>
    <Step n={2} title="Type your email and password">
        Enter the email your admin registered. Case doesn't matter — the
        portal lowercases the input before submitting. Click the eye icon
        on the password field to unhide what you're typing.
    </Step>
    <Step n={3} title="Click Grant Access">
        The button changes to *Authenticating…* while credentials are
        checked. On success, a toast reading **Access Granted** appears
        and you're forwarded straight to your dashboard.
    </Step>
</StepList>

<Callout kind="tip" title="Most browsers can save this login">
If your browser asks whether to save the password, accept — it's the
fastest way to skip typing on every visit.
</Callout>

## Troubleshooting

- **"Authentication Failed" toast.** Double-check the email is the one
  your admin registered. Passwords are case-sensitive — watch for a
  stuck Caps Lock.
- **"Access Denied" toast.** The account exists, but the role isn't
  `CLIENT`. Ask your admin to check the role assigned to your user.
- **Nothing happens when you click Grant Access.** A flaky network
  connection is the most common cause. Reload the page and try again.
- **You need to reset your password.** Use the
  <PortalLink to="/forgot-password" label="Forgot Password" /> flow from
  the login page — covered in the next article.
