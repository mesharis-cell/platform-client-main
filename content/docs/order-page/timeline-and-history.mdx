The status hero shows you what's happening _now_. The **status timeline** shows you everything that's happened up to now — one row per transition, timestamped, in chronological order.

## Where it lives

Scroll about a third of the way down the order page. The card is titled
**Order Timeline**.

## Reading a row

Each row shows four things:

- A **coloured dot** at the left-hand edge. The top row's dot is filled
  and ringed with primary — that's the current status. Everything below
  is muted.
- The **status name** — same labels you see everywhere else in the portal
  (IN_USE shows as "On Site" here, consistent with the status badge).
- The **date and time** of the transition, in your browser's local
  time zone.
- An optional **note** — when an admin adds a comment to a transition
  (most commonly on cancellation), it appears inline under the row.

## What it's useful for

Two main things:

- **Timeline disputes.** If you remember something different ("I thought
  the order was delivered Tuesday?"), the timeline has the authoritative
  record.
- **Audit trail.** For your own finance or ops reporting, the timeline
  is where you can see the lifecycle of the order without needing
  support to pull it for you.

<Callout kind="heads-up" title="The timeline doesn't auto-refresh">
    Like the rest of the order page, the timeline only refreshes when you reload. If you're watching
    for a status change in real time, the fastest signal is an email notification — [see which
    emails you receive](../reference/emails-you-receive) for the full list.
</Callout>

## Missing a status?

The timeline shows every transition _the system recorded_. A few
transitions are automatic and can happen within the same second — for
example, SUBMITTED → PRICING_REVIEW. You'll see just PRICING_REVIEW on
the timeline in that case, not a separate SUBMITTED row, because the
first was never persisted as a visible state.
