Sometimes an order has associated **service work** — maintenance on a damaged unit, rebranding on a panel, refurbishment between events. These live as **service requests**, separate from the order itself but linked to it so everyone can see the relationship.

<FeatureFlagNote flag="enable_service_requests" />

## Where it shows

On the order detail page, a card titled **Linked Service Requests** appears whenever your order has at least one service request attached.

## Per-card info

Each card shows three things:

- **Service request ID** in monospace — the one logistics references.
- **Operational status** as a badge — the stage in its own workflow
  (SUBMITTED, IN_REVIEW, APPROVED, IN_PROGRESS, COMPLETED, CANCELLED).
  This is separate from your order's own status.
- **Request type + total amount** — the category of service and the
  pricing (if any) attached to it.

Clicking anywhere on the card takes you to the service request's own
detail page where you can review its description, items, attachments,
and any quote it carries.

## Commercial vs operational status

Service requests can have two status lines running in parallel:

- The **operational status** — what's physically happening to the work.
- The **commercial status** — what's happening to the billing (INTERNAL,
  PENDING_QUOTE, QUOTED, QUOTE_APPROVED, INVOICED, PAID, CANCELLED).

The card on the order page shows the operational status. If the service
request is client-billable, you'll approve its quote from the service
request's own page, not from the order.

<Callout kind="tip" title="Service requests can block your order">
    A service request flagged as "blocks fulfillment" will hold your order from progressing past
    certain statuses until it's completed or a concession is applied. If your order seems stuck,
    check any linked service requests first.
</Callout>
