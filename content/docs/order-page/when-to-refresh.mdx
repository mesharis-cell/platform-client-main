A quick heads-up: the order page is a **point-in-time snapshot**. Once it's loaded, it doesn't listen for status changes or live-update. If the logistics team moves your order to IN_TRANSIT while you've got the page open, the page still shows CONFIRMED until you refresh.

## What updates on reload

Reloading the page (Cmd/Ctrl+R, or just clicking the browser back-and-forth) picks up:

- Current **order status** and its matching hero headline.
- **Quote state** (quoted / approved / declined) and the pricing breakdown.
- Any new **timeline entries** since you loaded.
- **Scan events** added to the fulfillment activity feed.
- New or changed **supporting documents** and **linked service requests**.

## When you'd actually need to refresh

Three realistic cases:

- You're expecting a **quote** (status is PRICING_REVIEW or
  PENDING_APPROVAL) and you got the "Quote Ready" email a minute ago.
- You're on **event day** and want to check if the truck is on the road
  yet (CONFIRMED → IN_TRANSIT transition).
- You **approved a quote** seconds ago and want to see the CONFIRMED
  state take effect.

## When you don't need to

Most of the time, you don't. The platform sends email notifications on
every status change you care about, so the inbox is the source of truth
for "is anything new?". See
[Emails you receive](../reference/emails-you-receive) for the full
notification list.

<Callout kind="tip" title="Use the email as the trigger">
Treat status-change emails as the cue to refresh if you need to. The
portal isn't going to push a bell-icon or toast to tell you — it just
silently updates the data when you reload.
</Callout>

## Why it works this way

Live updates would need a persistent connection (WebSockets or polling)
and introduce a class of bugs around stale state, reconciliation, and
"your page says X but the server says Y" confusion. The current design
trades that complexity for a simple rule: the page shows what the
server had the moment you loaded it, and email tells you when to
reload.
